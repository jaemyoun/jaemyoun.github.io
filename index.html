Cold Storage

Power-proportional large-scale object-based storage file systems and arrays in data centers

A data center that owns a massive storage infrastructure uses more than 150 million kilowatt-hours of energy and its power consumption is expected to double every year. Large-scale storage systems can consume 25% of the total power consumed by a data center. These considerations have motivated researchers to improve the energy efficiency of storage servers. One important goal is to re-design servers to be power-proportional, meaning that the power consumption is in step with the workload.

The workload that I am interested in is an mobile messenger service. The mobile messenger service is one of the most useful mobile applications for exchanging messages and photos with friends. The infrastructural needs of such services are rapidly growing with user demand for high-quality service, and high-end computing with sufficient resources are required to meet these demands.

Research topic #1Analyzing I/O patterns of image servers for the mobile messenger services
Research topic #2Establishment of Test Bed for the assessment of power management strategies in tiered storage systems
Research topic #3Benchmark framework development for large-scale object storage systems with customized multi-thread workloads
Research topic #4Design of distribution policy to improve energy efficiency of large-scale storage arrays
